Maximizing Life with a Minimalist Lifestyle
How many things do you need? A better question may be how many things do you even use? No, seriously, count them; just the essentials. Clothes, A bed to sleep in, a fridge to keep food in, maybe a microwave to cook with and utensils to eat with, a desk to study at, a desk lamp, a bike or car or some form of transportation, and probably the most commonly used: a laptop and phone. In some countries you’re fortunate to have the first two, but everything after that are the only things a person (in our society, anyways) could ever need.

April 17
America's Drive for Engineering is Broken
The year is 1969, Buzz Aldrin and Neil Armstrong are 238,900 miles away from home and less than a decade ago it was only a dream. In the next 41 months, there would be ten more Americans to explore the barren surface of the moon for a total of six landings. Then, in 1972, they stop. Man has never, and may never, travel farther, and since then, our flag has faded to white, bleached by the sun: somewhat of a metaphor for what many suggest is America's drive for progress today.
